Christopher Forsman Music.

I am proficient in both the role of a music producer and that of a live musician, specializing in the performance and recording of 1950s rock 'n' roll music. My repertoire includes both cover songs and original compositions. Additional audio samples can be found Futher down down the website for your reference.

Background

Ever since I was 11 years old, I've been drawn to the infectious tunes of the 50's rock 'n' roll. It all started when I heard the incredible local musician, Christian Westlin, sing and play Chuck Berry's legendary track, "Johnny B. Goode." I was so hooked that I had to find out more about that song as soon as I got home. That's when I discovered Chuck Berry and completely fell in love with this genre of music. I think I must have played "Johnny B. Goode" on repeat for an entire year, haha! It didn't take me long to explore other similar artists like Elvis Presley, Eddie Cochran, and Bill Haley. But it was when I heard Little Richard playing "Good Golly Miss Molly" on the piano that something inside me shifted. I knew right then and there that I was meant to be a pianist. I had already started tinkering on my grandparents' piano as soon as I could reach the keys, so I had a basic understanding of how it worked. However, it was around the age of 12 that I truly began dedicating myself to the instrument, pouring all my energy into playing.
